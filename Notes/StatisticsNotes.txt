Correlation:
Correlation is used to measure teh numerical relationship between two variables. A high correlation coefficient (close to 1), doesn't
mean that we can for sure conclude an actual relationship between two variables. Example:
  During summer, the sale of icecream at a beach increases. While at the same time the number of drowning accidents also increases.
This doesn't mean that the increase in ice cream sales, is the direct cause of the increased drowning accidents.
